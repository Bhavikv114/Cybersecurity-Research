My Journey Into Cybersecurity

My name is Bhavik Verma, and I am a high school student from India with a strong passion for cybersecurity and technology. My interest in cybersecurity was not something that appeared overnight. It developed gradually through curiosity, self-reflection, learning, and the desire to understand how technology shapes the modern world.

Like many students, I spent a significant amount of my time casually after schools reopened following the COVID-19 lockdown. During that period, I was not completely certain about my future career path. While many students around me already had clear goals of becoming engineers, doctors, or pursuing other traditional professions, I was still trying to discover what genuinely interested me and what kind of work would give me long-term satisfaction.

At the same time, I began hearing increasingly frequent news reports about cybercrime, ransomware attacks, data breaches, identity theft, online fraud, and large-scale security incidents affecting organizations around the world. Every time I read or watched such news, I found myself asking questions:

How did the attackers manage to break into these systems?
Why were the organizations unable to stop them?
What mistakes allowed the attack to happen?
How do cybersecurity professionals investigate and respond to these incidents?
How can similar attacks be prevented in the future?

These questions sparked a curiosity that I had never experienced before. Unlike many other subjects, cybersecurity made me want to search for answers on my own.

Discovering Whether Cybersecurity Was Truly for Me

When I was promoted to Class 9, I faced an important academic decision: choosing between Basic Mathematics and Standard Mathematics.

My father encouraged me to choose Standard Mathematics because it would keep more educational and career opportunities available in the future. However, before making that decision, I wanted to understand what I actually wanted to do with my life.

At that stage, I was unsure whether cybersecurity was a genuine passion or simply a field that sounded exciting because of the word "hacking." I did not want to make important decisions based only on temporary excitement. Instead, I wanted to determine whether this was a field I could genuinely see myself studying and working in for many years.

To find the answer, I began learning about cybersecurity independently.

I started exploring topics such as:

Computer networks
Operating systems
Linux
Cyber threats
Ethical hacking
Digital security
Internet technologies
Cyber defense strategies

As I spent more time learning, I realized that my interest was becoming stronger rather than fading away.

The Moment I Knew This Was My Path

One of the most important moments in my learning journey was experimenting with Linux distributions such as Kali Linux and exploring beginner cybersecurity learning resources.

For the first time, I was not simply using technology—I was trying to understand how it worked behind the scenes.

I learned how computers communicate through networks, how websites function, how vulnerabilities can be discovered, and how security professionals protect systems from malicious activities. Every new concept felt like solving a puzzle.

What attracted me most to cybersecurity was the combination of:

Technical knowledge
Problem-solving
Critical thinking
Continuous learning
Real-world impact

Cybersecurity challenges often require creativity, patience, and analytical thinking. I found great satisfaction in understanding complex systems and learning how they can be protected against threats.

That was the point at which I became confident that cybersecurity was not simply an interesting topic—it was a field that genuinely matched my interests and strengths.

Academic Decisions and Career Planning

Because of my growing passion for cybersecurity, I decided to choose Standard Mathematics in Class 9. Later, when selecting my stream in Class 11, I chose PCM (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics) to maintain eligibility for technology-related university programs and engineering pathways.

Like many Indian students interested in technical education, I also began preparing for the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE). While preparing for JEE, I spent considerable time researching universities, degree programs, and career opportunities related to cybersecurity.

During this research, I discovered that dedicated undergraduate cybersecurity programs in India were relatively limited compared to traditional engineering disciplines. Some programs were available, but many were expensive or did not provide the specialized cybersecurity-focused education I was seeking.

Since my goal has always been to pursue a Bachelor's degree specifically in Cyber Security, I expanded my search internationally.

Why I Chose Estonia and TalTech

My research eventually led me to Tallinn University of Technology (TalTech) in Estonia.

Several factors made Estonia particularly attractive to me:

Its reputation as one of the world's most digitally advanced countries
Strong national focus on cybersecurity and digital innovation
Home of the NATO Cooperative Cyber Defence Centre of Excellence
Thriving technology ecosystem in Tallinn
Strong international reputation in engineering and technology education

The more I learned about Estonia's digital transformation and cybersecurity initiatives, the more interested I became in studying there.

TalTech's strong academic reputation, modern curriculum, international environment, and focus on technology further reinforced my decision to pursue higher education in Estonia.

My Current Learning Journey

Today, I continue preparing for both JEE and my international university applications.

Alongside my academic studies, I actively work on improving my English proficiency through IELTS preparation and continue developing my cybersecurity knowledge through independent learning.

Some of the platforms and resources I use include:

TryHackMe
OpenLearn
Cybersecurity articles and research papers
Technical documentation
Industry reports and case studies

I believe that cybersecurity is a field where learning never stops. New technologies, threats, vulnerabilities, and defensive techniques emerge constantly, making continuous education essential.

Research and Future Goals

Recently, I have begun conducting independent cybersecurity research focused on topics such as:

Cyber attacks and attack methodologies
Security awareness
Threat intelligence
Defensive security strategies
Risk management
Emerging cybersecurity trends

Through this GitHub portfolio, I aim to document my learning journey, certifications, research projects, notes, practical exercises, and future cybersecurity projects.

My long-term goal is to become a cybersecurity professional who helps organizations defend their systems, protect sensitive information, and respond effectively to cyber threats. I hope to contribute to creating a safer and more secure digital world while continuously improving my technical and analytical skills.

Cybersecurity began as curiosity, grew into a passion, and has now become the career path I am determined to pursue.
